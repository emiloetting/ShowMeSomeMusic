Danceability

Depicted above is the danceability of chart entries from each year. Longitude represents a specific year, while altitude encodes chart position. Therefore, each tile provides information on a specific song within the Billboard Hot 100 Year-End Charts between 1959 and 2024.
Danceability is measured in values ranging from 0 to 1 and indicated by the brightness of the respective tile. The brighter the field, the more danceable its corresponding song. In some cases, data might be missing. The respective fields are then colored green.
Hover over a field enables a tooltip displaying basic information about the corresponding song it represents. By clicking the ball, its rotation will stop and it can be positioned and inspected to preference. Right click on a field opens up a field showing additional statistical information regarding the respective song's danceability compared to others. A click next to the ball will enable the rotation once again.

Bias Information:
Since not all tiles are of similar size, keep in mind that tile size does not contain or encode any extra information. Hence, if a specific area seems bright but small of size, remember that many songs might be encoded within that area of the ball. This effect occurs toward the top and bottom (fields closer to the ball's axis of rotation)!
